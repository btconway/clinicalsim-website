## The Challenge

EOL conversations are low-frequency, high-stakes events. Traditional training models cant provide the practice clinicians need.

## Key Insights

- Training solutions need to reach all learners, not only those with access to live standardized patients
- Every trainee deserves the same quality of practice opportunities
- Validated feedback mechanisms support measurable skill development
- Training should be available when and where learners need it

Recent developments in medical communication training continue to show the importance of structured, repeatable practice. Communication failures drive 40% of malpractice claims, and HCAHPS scores put millions in reimbursement at risk.
